HOW DOES THIS WORK?

When you enter a photograph in a SNAPATHON weekly competition you can win points in two ways:

  • 1. Your entry wins 1st, 2nd or 3rd place.
  • 2. You vote for the 1st, 2nd or 3rd placed photo.

Click below for a complete explanation of the rules and how to enter.


  • Review the competition rules

    The rules are as follows:

    • To get involved and enter competitions, you must first register as a member.
    • As a member you may enter 1 photograph into each weekly competition.
    • The photograph must be your own original creation.
    • The photograph must not depict anything of a violent, sexual or offensive nature.
    • The photograph must not be heavily edited. Only light retouching of exposure, contrast & white-balance is permitted.
    • The photograph must not violate the rights of any third party.
    • The photograph must not breach copyright law.
    • Entries are accepted through our online form each week from 0:00AM Monday until Friday at midnight.
    • Voting takes place from Saturday morning at 0:00 until 22:00PM Sunday.
    • Winners are announced on the "Recent Winners" page and all points & awards are automatically assigned.
    • If you enter a photograph into competition, you MUST vote for 1 photograph (you cannot vote for your own image).
    • If you fail to vote between Saturday morning at 0:00 and Sunday at 22:00, your entry will be nullified and its points/votes set to 0.
    • All times quoted are UTC+00:00
    • Scoring is based on the number of votes an image receives from other members. The photo which receives the highest number of votes wins a 1st place gold award badge and its' creator receives 7 points. The next placed photo receives a 2nd place silver award badge and its' creator receives 5 points. Finally the 3rd placed photo receives a bronze award badge and its' creator receives 3 points.
    • Users can also win points by voting for winning images. If a user votes for the 1st placed photo, that user receives 3 points. If a user votes for the 2nd placed photo, that user receives 2 points. Finally if a user votes for the 3rd placed photo, that user receives 1 point.
    • It follows that the highest number of points a user can receive per competition is 9 (by winning 1st place and voting for the 2nd place image).
